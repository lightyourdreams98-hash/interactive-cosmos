Comet Explorer

1. Nucleus (The Core)

This is like a dirty snowball in space! It is made of ice, rock, and dust all frozen together. It is usually about 1-10 kilometers wide.

2. Coma (The Cloud)

When the comet gets close to the Sun, the heat makes the ice turn into gas. This creates a big glowing cloud around the nucleus!

3. Dust Tail (Yellow Trail)

Sunlight pushes tiny dust particles away from the comet. This creates a curved, yellowish tail like smoke!

4. Ion Tail (Blue Trail)

The solar wind (like a breeze from the Sun) blows gas particles away in a straight line. This makes a blue tail that always points away from the Sun!

Amazing Fact!

Comet tails can be millions of kilometers long - longer than the distance from Earth to the Moon!

Did You Know?

The tails always point AWAY from the Sun, even when the comet is moving away from it!
