ü™® Crust

The outermost rocky layer with distinct thickness variations between near and far sides.

Thickness (Near Side): 30-40 km
Thickness (Far Side): 50-60 km
Composition: Oxygen, silicon, magnesium, iron, calcium, aluminum
Features: Impact craters, volcanic domes, rilles, wrinkle ridges

üåã Mantle

The thick middle layer, mostly solid with some partially molten zones near the core.

Depth: Extends to ~1,000 km
State: Mostly solid with partial melt zones
Composition: Silicate minerals (olivine, pyroxene) rich in Mg and Fe
Activity: Moonquakes detected, related to Earth's tidal stresses

üåë New Moon

Moon is between Earth and Sun. The side facing Earth is dark.

üåí Waxing Crescent

A thin crescent of light appears and grows larger each night.

üåì First Quarter

Half the Moon is illuminated (right side in Northern Hemisphere).

üåî Waxing Gibbous

More than half is illuminated and continues increasing.

üåï Full Moon

The entire face visible from Earth is fully illuminated.

üåñ Waning Gibbous

Illumination begins to decrease after the full moon.

üåó Last Quarter

Half is lit again, but opposite side compared to first quarter.

üåò Waning Crescent

The light shrinks to a thin crescent before becoming new again.
